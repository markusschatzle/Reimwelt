# Product

## Register

product

## Users

German-language writers, poets, lyricists, and language learners who need to find rhyming words quickly. They use the tool in the middle of creative work — often on desktop, occasionally mobile — and want results fast without friction. The primary task on any screen is: type a word, get a clear list of rhyming candidates.

## Product Purpose

Reimwelt.de is a rhyme-search engine for German and other languages. It queries a linguistic database by phonetic ending (IPA), lets users filter by syllable count, meter, and part of speech, and surfaces the most useful matches ranked by rhyme quality and word frequency. Success means a writer finds a usable rhyme in under 10 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, literary. The tool should feel like a well-designed reference book: authoritative but not cold, functional but typographically considered. It does not shout or decorate. It gets out of the way and lets the language be the focus.

## Anti-references

- Flashy SaaS dashboards with hero colors and gradient cards
- Generic search engines (Google, Bing aesthetic)
- Over-designed dictionary apps with excessive illustration or animation
- Dark-mode-by-default developer tools
- Rose/crimson accent colors that draw eye away from results

## Design Principles

1. **Content is the UI.** The word list is the product. Everything else — header, filters, panel — serves it. Never compete with the results.
2. **One thing at a time.** Search → results → detail. The interface should feel linear and deliberate, not cluttered with simultaneous affordances.
3. **Restrained brand presence.** One muted accent color, used sparingly. Background and surface carry no decorative color. Typography does the heavy lifting.
4. **Readable above all.** Body text contrast, spacing, and line-height are non-negotiable. If a font choice or color saves one line but hurts legibility, choose legibility.
5. **Functional elegance.** Animations exist to confirm state changes (copy feedback, panel slide), not to add personality. Every interactive element has a clear visual affordance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as minimum target. Text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 for body, ≥ 3:1 for large text. All interactive elements keyboard-accessible. Reduced-motion respected for animations. German as primary language; interface copy in German.
